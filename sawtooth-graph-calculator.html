TL;DR: In the automotive engineering and racing world, this is almost universally known as a “Sawtooth Graph” or “Sawtooth Chart” because of the jagged, teeth-like shape the lines make.

More formally, depending on the specific engineering context, you might also hear it referred to as:

  • A Gearing Chart — the most common functional name used by transmission builders and chassis shops when selecting rear-end ratios.
  • A Shift Diagram — used by calibrators and tuners to visualize where a transmission will land after a shift.
  • An RPM vs. Speed Graph — the literal, mathematical description of the axes.

Engineers and race teams use these sawtooth graphs to visually verify that a specific transmission and rear-end combination will keep an engine inside its optimal powerband. If the “teeth” of the saw drop too low, it tells the builder that the gear spacing is too wide and the engine will bog down after a shift.
